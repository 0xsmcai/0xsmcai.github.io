What a Zero-Human Company Means in Practice

A Zero-Human Company is a business where AI agents handle nearly all operational work — refining ideas, writing and auditing code, deploying contracts, responding to market signals, and keeping everything running smoothly. A human or governance layer sets the initial direction and constraints, but after that the agents manage execution.

Why the Traditional Startup Launch Model Is Breaking Down

For years the standard path stayed the same: someone comes up with an idea, writes a deck or whitepaper, raises money, hires a team, spends months building, and then hopes the product gains traction. That model assumed building quality products was slow and expensive and that only humans could do it well.

Those assumptions no longer hold true. Agents can now write, test, audit, and deploy solid code in just days. The real limitation today is the outdated infrastructure around launches — platforms and processes still designed for slow human timelines.
